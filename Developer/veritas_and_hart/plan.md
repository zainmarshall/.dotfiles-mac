# Veritas & Hart, LLC — Website Attributes Brief

*For designer reference — April 2026*

---

## 1. Brand Identity

**Legal Name:** Veritas & Hart, LLC

**Name Meaning:** Veritas = truth (Latin; also a nod to Harvard). Hart = heart, empathy, courage (Old English). The ampersand binds strategy and humanity together — the two things that define how the firm operates.

**Founder:** Ummara (Mara) Marshall, Founder & Principal

**Tagline Territory** (choose one or adapt):
- "Results that endure. Values that hold."
- "Serious work, done with care."
- "I help organizations navigate change — honestly, carefully, and together."

**Positioning in one sentence:** Veritas & Hart partners with organizations navigating complex change — from strategy through execution — with senior-level attention, honest counsel, and genuine care for the people doing the work.

---

## 2. Core Values (Non-Negotiable in Tone and Content)

These four values should be felt throughout the site — in the copy, the visual language, the pacing, and the overall experience. They are not a section to list; they are the site's personality.

- **Honesty.** The firm tells clients what it actually sees, even when it's hard to hear — because that's what moves organizations forward.
- **Partnership.** The work is done *with* people, not above them. Change doesn't stick unless the people living it are brought along.
- **Craft.** Whatever is delivered is done with rigor and care. The work will be excellent.
- **Care.** Genuine investment in the success of the organization *and* its people — not just the project.

**Critical brand guardrail:** The site must never position the firm by diminishing competitors or large consultancies. No "unlike the big firms" or "what others promise and don't deliver." The brand is built entirely on what Veritas & Hart *is* — not what others aren't.

---

## 3. Target Audience

The site must speak credibly to a broad range of buyers, not just Fortune 500:

- **C-suite and senior executives** at large enterprises navigating transformation, M&A integration, or AI adoption
- **Mid-market and PE-backed companies** scaling operations or building infrastructure for the first time
- **HR and Talent leaders** seeking compensation strategy, job architecture, or organizational design support
- **Anyone evaluating Mara after a referral, LinkedIn visit, or Google search** — the site must serve as an instant credibility check

The tone should feel like a conversation with a senior peer — warm, direct, confident without being corporate.

---

## 4. Site Roles (It Must Do All Three)

1. **Credibility check:** When someone Googles "Veritas & Hart" or "Ummara Marshall," they land here and immediately understand this is a serious, values-driven firm with real operating experience.
2. **Lead generation:** Clear path to starting a conversation. No friction. No "submit a form and wait." The site should feel like an open door.
3. **Engagement showcase:** Enough specificity about the kind of work done — and the results achieved — that a prospective client can self-identify: "this is what I need."

---

## 5. Recommended Site Structure

### Hero / Above the Fold
- Firm name and tagline
- One-sentence positioning statement
- Primary call-to-action: "Begin the Conversation" or similar
- Impact stats for immediate credibility (see Section 7)

### Philosophy / About
- The meaning behind "Veritas & Hart" — truth and heart as operating principles, not aspirational brand values
- Mara's background told through the lens of what it means for the client: Amazon, Marriott, investment banking, Harvard — framed as pattern recognition and real operating experience, not a resume recitation
- The "firm of one" positioning: the senior person is the person who does the work, every time. Frame as a deliberate choice, not a limitation.

### Services
Three pillars, each with a short description and example outcomes:

**AI Transformation & Governance** — From strategy to scaled adoption. Business case development, governance frameworks, vendor roadmaps, Board-level presentations, adoption programs.

**Operational Strategy & Excellence** — Complex operations simplified and strengthened. Process redesign, cross-functional alignment, KPI architecture, revenue acceleration. Lean Six Sigma discipline applied with strategic clarity.

**Strategic Program Leadership** — Enterprise programs that land. End-to-end ownership from research infrastructure to executive deliverables. Stakeholder alignment, change management, and measurement built in from day one.

*Note: These should not feel rigid. The site should communicate that engagements are shaped around the client's actual need — not forced into a pre-packaged offering.*

### Impact / Results
- Quantified proof points (see Section 7)
- Brief, anonymized or generalized case snapshots showing the *kind* of work, not just the brand names
- Sectors served: technology, hospitality, healthcare, robotics, financial services

### Contact
- Simple, inviting. "Start a Conversation" framing — not "Request a Proposal"
- Direct email and/or scheduling link
- Washington, D.C. Metro area noted

## 6. Visual and Tonal Attributes

### Design Feel
- **Executive presence** — the site should feel like walking into a well-appointed office, not a startup landing page
- **Refined editorial luxury** — think deep, warm, grounded. Not flashy, not minimalist-cold, not corporate-gray.
- **Warmth and substance simultaneously** — the design must carry both gravitas and approachability

### Color Direction
The February prototype used deep forest green (#1A3C2E), warm gold (#C9A84C), and cream (#FAF7F0). This palette tested well and reflects the brand: grounded, warm, principled. Designer should feel free to evolve this, but the emotional territory — deep warmth, not corporate blue/gray — should hold.

### Typography Direction
Serif for headlines (authority, tradition, trust), clean sans-serif for body (clarity, modernity). The February prototype used Cormorant Garamond + Jost. The pairing worked — elegant without being precious.

### Photography / Imagery
- If using photos: real, warm, natural light. No stock-photo handshakes or skyline shots.
- Abstract or textural alternatives work well — think linen, stone, botanical, architectural detail
- Avoid anything that feels generic-consultant or Silicon Valley

### Copy Tone
- First person is fine — this is a founder-led firm and Mara's voice *is* the brand
- Warm, direct, confident. No jargon. No buzzwords unless they're earned.
- Sentences should feel like how Mara actually talks to a senior leader: clear, respectful, unhurried
- Every claim should be grounded in something real — no vague superlatives

## 8. What the Site Should NOT Be

- **Not a resume.** Career history supports credibility but the site is about what the firm does for *clients*, not a chronological career story.
- **Not a blog (yet).** Unless Mara is ready to commit to regular content, don't build a blog section that sits empty. A "Perspectives" section can be added later when there's content to sustain it.
- **Not a services catalog.** The three pillars provide structure, but the real message is: "tell me your problem and I'll build the right engagement around it."
- **Not comparative.** Zero references to what other firms do or don't do. The brand stands on its own.
- **Not overbuilt.** A single-page site with clear sections, or a very tight multi-page site (Home, About, Services, Contact), is better than a sprawling site with thin pages. Quality over quantity.

---

## 9. Technical Notes for the Designer

- The site must be mobile-responsive — many executive buyers will first encounter it on a phone after receiving a referral
- Fast load times matter. Keep animations tasteful and purposeful, not decorative.
- SEO basics: the site should be discoverable for "Ummara Marshall," "Veritas & Hart," and relevant service terms
- A simple contact form or direct email/calendar link is sufficient — no complex intake forms
- Consider a downloadable one-pager or capabilities overview as a future add-on
